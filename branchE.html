COMMANDS

push [-p|--patch] [-k|--[no-]keep-index] [-u|--include-untracked] [-a|--all] [-q|--quiet] [-m|--message ] [--pathspec-from-file= [--pathspec-file-nul]] [--] […​] Save your local modifications to a new stash entry and roll them back to HEAD (in the working tree and in the index). The part is optional and gives the description along with the stashed state. For quickly making a snapshot, you can omit "push". In this mode, non-option arguments are not allowed to prevent a misspelled subcommand from making an unwanted stash entry. The two exceptions to this are stash -p which acts as alias for stash push -p and pathspec elements, which are allowed after a double hyphen -- for disambiguation.
